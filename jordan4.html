Jordan 4 Sneakers

The Jordan 4, released in 1989, is a legendary sneaker that played a significant role in the Air Jordan franchise. Designed by Tinker Hatfield, it was the first Air Jordan to feature a visible plastic netting, a design element that would go on to become a hallmark of the brand. The Jordan 4 was also the first to feature the iconic "Nike Air" branding on the sole.

  • Mid-top silhouette with premium leather and mesh materials
  • Visible Air cushioning in the sole for maximum comfort
  • Iconic plastic netting on the sides for added ventilation
  • Distinctive Jumpman logo and "Nike Air" branding on select releases
  • Worn by Michael Jordan during his memorable 1989 playoff game-winning shot against the Cleveland Cavaliers
